NATIONAL CADET CORPS

The National Cadet Corps (NCC) is the youth wing of the Indian Armed Forces, with its headquarters in New Delhi, India.

  1. Formation and Purpose: - The NCC was formed in 1948 and can be traced back to the 'University Corps' created under the Indian Defence Act 1917. - It aims to develop character, comradeship, discipline, and a secular outlook among young individuals. - NCC recruits students from schools, colleges, and universities.
  2. Tri-Services Organization: - The NCC is a voluntary organization that operates as a Tri-Services Organization, comprising the Army, Navy, and Air Force. - cadets receive basic military training in small arms and drill.
  3. Motto and Principles: - The motto of the NCC is"Unity and Discipline" (Ekta aur Anushasan). - Four principles guide NCC cadets: - Obey with a smile. - Be punctual. - Work hard and without fuss. - Make no excuses and tell no lies.
  4. History: - The first NCC unit was raised in Delhi in 1948, presided over by Prime Minister Jawaharlal Nehru. - The NCC Day is traditionally celebrated on the last Sunday of November.
  5. Size and Structure: - The NCC has around 10,00,000 to 13,00,000 cadets. - It operates in schools and colleges across India. - Cadets have no liability for active military service after completing their course.
  6. Engagements: - The NCC has participated in various events, including the Indo-Pakistani war of 1965 and the Bangladesh-Pakistani war of 1971.
